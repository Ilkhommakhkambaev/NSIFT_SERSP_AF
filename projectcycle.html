Planning Pipeline — From Data to Decisions
How community needs become funded subprojects. This is the core of the methodology.

Village Profile

Baseline snapshot of each village — demographics, infrastructure, services, economic activities, vulnerabilities, climate risks. The "diagnosis" before any prescription.

Led by: VPC with CDA support · 1 week per village
→ 45 village profiles submitted to JPC and NSIFT

Climate Risk Assessment

Climate Ambassadors identify hazards specific to each village: GLOFs (glacial lake outburst floods), landslides, drought. Participatory risk mapping, historical hazard review, adaptation priorities.

Led by: Climate Ambassadors with VPC
→ Findings feed into Village Profile and VDP. Must NOT be standalone.

Focus Group Discussions (FGDs)

6 FGDs per village, each with a different group: women, youth, elders, entrepreneurs, vulnerable households, professionals. Each group identifies and prioritizes their needs.

Led by: VPC with CDA facilitation · 6 groups × 45 villages = 270 FGDs
→ FGD reports with prioritized needs. Women/youth priorities tracked separately.

Village Prioritization Meeting (VPM)

Whole village meets. Each FGD group presents priorities (5 min each). Community discusses. Climate Ambassadors present climate considerations. Village votes on top priority. Democratic selection.

Led by: VPC · Attendance: 60% households, 50% women
→ VPM minutes, documented vote results, selected village priority

Village Development Plan (VDP)

Pulls together: village profile + climate assessment + FGD results + voted priority. Includes technical specs, budget estimate, climate considerations, gender/youth priorities, O&M plan.

Prepared by: VPC with TFP support
→ 45 VDPs submitted to JPC. Must align with government planning formats.

Jamoat Development Plan (JDP)

JPC takes all VDPs from its jamoat, reviews against criteria, scores and ranks subprojects, allocates available funds, documents rejected proposals with reasons. Packages everything into one plan.

Led by: JPC with TFP support
→ 6 JDPs. Publicly displayed. Submitted to district → NSIFT → WB for approval.
WB emphasis: Village profiles and VDPs must feed into the government's own planning system — not just exist as project documents. The JDP should inform district socio-economic plans. WB wants to see concrete mechanisms for how this happens, not just statements of intent.
Accountability — 3-Phase Social Audits
Youth Monitors lead these. Three audits at critical milestones.
0%

Phase 1: After Mobilization

When: After VPC/JPC selection, before construction
Focus:
• Was selection process inclusive?
• Were women/youth adequately represented?
• Was information publicly disclosed?
• Did community understand the process?

Output: Phase 1 audit report + action plan for improvements
50%

Phase 2: Construction Midpoint

When: ~50% of construction complete
Focus:
• Quality meeting specifications?
• Budget on track?
• Gender inclusion in labor?
• Timeline progress?
• Issues identified?

Output: Phase 2 audit report + corrective action plan if needed
100%

Phase 3: Completion

When: Construction complete
Focus:
• Final quality acceptable?
• All work completed per design?
• O&M committee ready?
• Community satisfied?
• Lessons learned?

Output: Phase 3 audit report + community sign-off + handover authorization
Other Accountability Mechanisms

Grievance Mechanism

NSIFT-operated. Village → Jamoat → District → Central. 15 days investigation, 30 days resolution. ⚠ WB says GRM is NSIFT's responsibility, not TFP's.

Public Disclosure

All VDPs, JDP, budgets, contracts, progress reports, audit results posted publicly at jamoat, villages, and NSIFT website.

Community Scorecards

Semi-annual satisfaction surveys on participation quality, service delivery, responsiveness.

Cross-Cutting Themes — Throughout Everything
These run across all phases, not just one section.

Gender Inclusion

50% women in VPC. Equal gender in YM and CA. Women's priorities tracked separately in FGDs. Women in construction labor. SEA/SH prevention.

Youth Participation

50% of each gender under age threshold (⚠ under review). YM and CA are youth-led. Youth priorities in VDP and JDP. Youth perspectives in all planning.

Climate Mainstreaming

CA throughout: risk assessment → VDP integration → subproject screening → monitoring. Climate adaptation in all infrastructure designs. Not standalone.

Component 2 Coordination

Livelihoods orientation in all meetings. Application form distribution. Infrastructure-livelihood linkages. Handover to Implementing Partner when selected.

Government Alignment

Village profiles → VDPs → JDPs must feed into government planning. Not just project documents. WB wants concrete mechanisms for uptake.

Special Tracking (WB Required)

Track: (1) VPC/JPC members who enter local government; (2) Community priorities funded by other sources beyond the project. Report on these as KPIs.

WB emphasis: Sana wants outcome-focused KPIs with a proper monitoring plan and frequency — not just a list of activities. Including: number of VDPs/JDPs taken up by government, subproject outcomes (students benefiting, jobs created, etc.), and MIS upload schedule.
